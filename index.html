01

Rotation

The engine of distance. Hip-to-torso separation creates real clubhead speed.

02

Mobility

Flexibility with control. The positions your swing needs — owned and repeatable.

03

Flexibility

The range to load, coil, and fire without strain or compensation.

04

Strength

The engine behind posture, stability, and ground force production.

The mental system

6 Pillars of Golf Mental Performance

The mental side of golf is not about staying positive. It is a structured internal operating system — built session by session alongside your physical training.

1

Clarity

Define exactly what you want and why it matters. Direction over guessing.

2

Identity

Become the golfer who naturally has what you want. Your game rises to your identity.

3

Standards

Set the rules you hold yourself to — in training and on the course.

4

Execution

Daily action with discipline. The practice of showing up and following through.

5

Results

Use feedback to improve — not to judge. Read your game and adjust intelligently.

6

Leadership

Take full ownership. Lead yourself first. That is how improvement compounds.

Questions

Frequently asked

Do I need a gym or special equipment?

No. Most of the program can be done with minimal equipment — a resistance band, a pair of dumbbells, and a medicine ball cover the majority of exercises. Many movements are bodyweight or require only a doorway or a wall.

How much time does each session take?

30 to 40 minutes per session. Four sessions per week. The schedule is designed around your golf — Monday, Tuesday, Thursday, and Friday — leaving the weekend free for playing. If those days do not work, the 2-on 1-off 2-on rhythm is what matters, not the specific days.

What is the mental training component?

Every session ends with a 10-minute guided mental module. You take a notebook, answer three specific prompts, and write a personal commitment. Over four weeks this builds Clarity, Identity, Standards, Execution, Results, and Leadership — the six mental pillars that hold your game together under pressure.

Is this for beginners or experienced golfers?

Both. The program is designed as a diagnostic as much as a training plan. It reveals what your body can do, what it cannot do yet, and what your game specifically needs. Beginners build a foundation. Experienced golfers uncover the physical and mental gaps that have been limiting them.

What format do I receive the documents in?

Both documents are delivered as professional PDF files — instant download after purchase. They work on any device: phone, tablet, laptop, or printed. You can take them to the gym, to the course, or anywhere you train.

What happens after the 4 weeks?

The closing section of the program is a personal diagnostic — four questions that reveal exactly what your body and mind need next. That roadmap points directly to the 12-Week and 20-Week programs when you are ready to go deeper.
